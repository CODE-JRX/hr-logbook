2. Purpose of the System

The System is implemented by the ASIST Human Resource Management Office (HRMO) for the sole purpose of:

  • Automating and securing daily time and attendance logging.
  • Enhancing the accuracy and efficiency of attendance records.
  • Reducing time fraud (e.g., buddy-punching).
  • Streamlining administrative processes related to attendance monitoring.

3. Data Collection, Use, and Storage

3.1. Type of Data Collected

The System will capture and process your biometric data, specifically a digital template (a mathematical representation) of your facial features. It does not store actual photographs or video footage for logging purposes.

3.2. How Data is Used

Your facial template will be used solely for verifying your identity when logging in (clocking in/out) and for generating attendance reports.

3.3. Data Storage & Security

  • Biometric templates are encrypted and stored on a secure server controlled by the HRMO/[Company Name].
  • Data is not shared with, sold to, or accessed by any third parties without your explicit consent, except as required by law.
  • We implement reasonable technical and organizational measures to protect your biometric data from unauthorized access, disclosure, or loss.

4. User Consent and Rights

4.1. Voluntary Consent

Your provision of biometric data is voluntary. However, refusal to provide such data will necessitate the use of an alternative, non-biometric method of time logging as determined by the HRMO.

4.2. Right to Information

You have the right to be informed about how your biometric data is handled.

4.3. Data Deletion

Upon termination of your employment, or at your written request (subject to HR policies), your biometric template will be permanently deleted from the active System, unless retention is required by applicable law.

5. User Responsibilities

  • You must use the System personally. Any attempt to log attendance for another person (e.g., using a photograph, mask, or proxy) is strictly prohibited and constitutes a serious violation of company policy.
  • You are responsible for positioning yourself correctly in front of the recognition device to ensure accurate reading.
  • You must immediately report any issues with the System (e.g., failure to recognize, hardware problems) to the HRMO or IT support.
  • You must not attempt to tamper with, hack, or disrupt the System hardware or software.
